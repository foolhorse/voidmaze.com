How we use cookies and similar technologies

Cookies

A cookie is a plain text file stored on a computer or mobile device by a web server. The content of the cookie is only retrieved or read by the server that created it. Each cookie is unique to your web browser or mobile application. Cookies often contain identifiers, site names, etc.

We sometimes store cookies on your device. We enable cookies to improve user experience, including login and verification, storage of your preferences and settings, statistical analysis, interest-based advertising, etc. You can manage, delete, and disable cookies according to your preferences. However, clearing or disabling cookies may affect all or part of your use of the services we provide.

Web beacon/pixel tag

In addition to cookies, we also use web beacons or pixel tags on our website. A web beacon is typically an electronic image embedded in a website or email that can be used to recognize cookies on your device while you are viewing the webpage or email. Pixel tags allow us to deliver emails in a format that you can read and enable us to know if an email is opened. We use these technologies for a variety of purposes, including to analyze service usage (in conjunction with cookies) and to provide content and advertisements that are more relevant to you.
